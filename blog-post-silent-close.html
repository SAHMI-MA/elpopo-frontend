The Silent Close: Why Talking Less Wins More Tours

Tarik El Mazani June 19, 2026 6 min read
Back to Blog

The moment right after the ask is the most fragile point in the entire tour — and it's the moment most reps ruin by filling it with words. Silence feels uncomfortable, so they rescue the buyer from it, and in doing so, they rescue the buyer right out of the decision.

The discomfort you feel after asking isn't a signal that something's wrong. It's usually the sound of a real decision being made. Interrupting it with a follow-up sentence doesn't ease the tension — it just hands the buyer a new topic to hide behind.

Whoever speaks first after the ask usually gives away the position they were trying to hold.

This isn't about being rigid or cold. It's about trusting the process enough to let the buyer arrive at their own answer, instead of being talked into — or out of — one. The reps who master this aren't more aggressive. They're more patient, at exactly the moment patience is hardest.

Knowing exactly how long to hold, what to watch for, and how to respond the moment they speak first is the discipline The Closer's Code was written to install.
